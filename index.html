About Me

A Singaporean
in Berlin

"Connections are the currency of the future — and I invest in people first."
FinTech Sales & Partnerships Asia–Europe Community Builder Run Club Connector

Hi, I'm Andralynn Tan Wei Qing — a Singaporean currently based in Berlin, Germany, where I work as a Manager in Sales and Partnerships. My career has been shaped by a deep love for fintech, a relentless curiosity about how finance and technology intersect, and a genuine belief in the power of human connection.

My journey began in Singapore, where I first discovered fintech during my polytechnic years at Ngee Ann Polytechnic. I served as President of the school's FinTech club and became a Founding Vice President of the Singapore FinTech Youth Chapter (SFYC) — launching national-level events and industry programmes at the Singapore FinTech Festival when I was just 17.

While pursuing my Bachelor of Business Management at Singapore Management University (SMU), I joined Elevandi (now the Global Finance & Technology Network, established by MAS) as an intern. I completed a full rotation across Marketing, Partnerships, Business Development, and Content teams — and undertook a 6-month internship posting in China, building cross-border relationships across Asia.

After graduating from SMU in December 2025, I made the bold move to Berlin — a city I chose for its vibrant fintech ecosystem, its position as a bridge between East and West, and its energy as one of Europe's most dynamic startup hubs. Today, I focus on building global corridors between Asia and Europe, connecting people, capital, and ideas across borders.

Beyond work, I'm passionate about running (I once hit a 4:58 min/km pace!), building community, and helping others navigate the world of fintech and finance.

The Move

🇸🇬 Singapore
→ 🇩🇪 Berlin

December 2025. Graduation cap barely off. Bags packed. Andralynn boarded a one-way flight from Singapore to Berlin — a city she had never lived in, in a country she was still learning to call home.

It wasn't a spontaneous decision. It was the culmination of years of intentional career-building — from the corridors of MAS and the global stages of the Singapore FinTech Festival, to cross-border postings in China and late-night conversations with fintech leaders from every corner of the world.

Berlin called because it sits at the intersection of everything she loves: a thriving European startup scene, a growing fintech ecosystem, and a city that rewards curiosity, ambition, and grit. As one of Europe's most dynamic hubs, Berlin is where the next chapter of her story is being written.

Today, she bridges Asia and Europe — bringing her deep roots in Singapore's MAS and GFTN ecosystem to the heart of the European fintech landscape, one partnership at a time.
